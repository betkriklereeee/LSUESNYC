Master Key Systems — Designed & Installed in NYC

Control who has access to what across your building or property — without handing out a different key for every door. We design, cut, and install custom master key systems tailored to your layout and access requirements.

How a master key system works

A master key system allows multiple locks — each with its own individual key — to also be opened by a single master key that operates all of them. The system is built around a pinning matrix: each cylinder is pinned so that its individual key works only that lock, while the master key satisfies the shear line across every cylinder in the system.

Systems can be tiered across multiple levels — a grand master key for ownership, sub-master keys for managers or superintendents, and individual keys for tenants or staff. Each tier controls exactly the doors it needs to, nothing more.

Access tiers — an example structure

Grand Master

Building ownership / management company

Opens every lock in the system — all units, all common areas, all service doors. Typically held by one or two people only.

Master

Superintendent / building manager

Opens common areas, utility rooms, and mechanical spaces — but not individual residential units without authorization.

Sub-Master

Floor managers / section leads

Opens a defined subset of doors — useful for multi-floor buildings or properties with distinct zones or departments.

Change Key

Individual tenants / staff

Opens only the specific lock assigned to that person. Cannot open any other door in the system.

Who we design systems for

Multi-unit residential buildings

Co-ops, condos, and rental buildings — tiered access for management, super, and individual tenants without key proliferation.

Commercial offices

Open-plan offices, private suites, and server rooms — different access levels for executives, staff, and facilities teams.

Schools & institutions

Restricted access by floor, department, or role — with a clear hierarchy that can be updated without rekeying the entire building.

Landlords & property managers

Manage access across multiple properties or units from a single key — with individual tenant keys that can be changed independently.
